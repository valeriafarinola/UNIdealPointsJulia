# Replication log

## Goal

Replicate the UN voting ideal point estimator from Bailey, Strezhnev, and Voeten (2017), starting from the original R/Rcpp implementation.

The final objective is to reproduce the estimated ideal points and replicate the figure showing the ideal points of the five permanent members of the UN Security Council.

## Step 1: R smoke test

I first ran the original `UN_Ideal_Rcpp.R` script with reduced MCMC settings:

- K = 200
- Burn = 50
- Thin = 10

The objective was not substantive replication, but verification that:

- the Rcpp code compiled correctly;
- the data loaded correctly;
- the MCMC loop executed;
- output files were generated.

The script successfully produced output files in the `Output/` folder.

## Step 2: Validation of estimated ideal points

I loaded `IdealpointestimatesAll_Apr2020.csv` in R and plotted the ideal points for the five permanent members of the UN Security Council.

The resulting trajectories appeared substantively plausible:
- USA strongly positive;
- USSR/Russia negative during the Cold War;
- UK and France close to the USA;
- China entering later in the series.

## Step 3: Initial Julia translation

I created a Julia workspace and translated the Rcpp function `PopulateThetaVector.cpp`.

The Julia version was validated:
- on a toy example;
- on the real matrices generated by the R code.

## Step 4: Translation of theta update

I translated the deterministic component of the theta update into Julia:
- lagged theta prior;
- posterior mean (`ThetaMean`);
- posterior variance (`VarTheta`).

I then implemented:
- Gaussian random draw;
- truncation to [-5, 5];
- normalization to mean 0 and standard deviation 1.
## Step 5: Integrated Julia theta-update script

I created a structured Julia project with:

- `src/UNIdealPointsJulia.jl`
- `src/populate_theta_vector.jl`
- `src/theta_update.jl`
- `src/lag_prior.jl`
- `scripts/load_inputs.jl`
- `scripts/reproduce_theta_step.jl`

The script `reproduce_theta_step.jl` loads the intermediate R output files, reconstructs the lagged theta prior, computes `ThetaMean` and `VarTheta`, draws a new theta vector, normalizes it, and maps country-session theta values to the observation-level `ThetaVector`.

The script successfully produced:

- `Length Theta: 6443`
- `Mean Theta: approximately 0`
- `Std Theta: 1`
